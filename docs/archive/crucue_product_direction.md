# Crucue Product Direction

## What is Crucue?

Crucue is a **private B2C mobile app for caregivers and loved ones**. It helps users support the people they care about through daily challenges, better communication, calmer routines, guided action plans, reflection, and follow-up.

---

## Who is it for?

- **Parents** caring for children (young children, teens, neurodivergent)
- **Adults** caring for aging or unwell parents
- **Partners** or family members supporting loved ones
- Anyone who wants to be a more thoughtful, effective caregiver

---

## The core problem we solve

Caregiving is hard. When something difficult happens — a meltdown, a conflict, a hard moment — most people don't know what to do. They guess, they react, they feel alone.

Crucue gives them:
1. A calm, practical plan for right now
2. Context grounded in their specific person
3. A way to remember what worked
4. Ongoing guidance as situations evolve

---

## Product principles

### 1. Private by default
Every care profile is private, encrypted, and never shared. Crucue is a personal tool, not a social platform.

### 2. Supportive, not prescriptive
Crucue offers guidance, not instructions. Users always know what's right for their situation.

### 3. Grounded in the person
The better you describe your loved one, the more specific and useful the plans become.

### 4. Honest about limits
Crucue is not a substitute for professional medical, psychological, or therapeutic care. We say this clearly and consistently.

### 5. Calm and clear
No unnecessary complexity. No pushy notifications. No manipulative design patterns.

---

## Core user journey (MVP)

1. **Onboard** — learn what Crucue does in 3 screens
2. **Create account** — private, email-based
3. **Create a care profile** — name, relationship, what helps, what to avoid
4. **Log a daily challenge** — describe what happened
5. **Get a support plan** — AI-generated, structured, and actionable
6. **Save tasks** — check off steps from the plan
7. **Follow-up chat** — continue the conversation with Crucue
8. **Reflect** — check in after trying the plan
9. **See insights** — weekly summary of patterns and what worked
10. **Build over time** — your profiles get richer, plans get smarter

---

## Language and tone guide

### Use:
- loved one, child, parent, partner, family member
- care profile, support plan, daily challenge, routine, check-in
- what helped, next steps, gentle guidance, calm moment
- "This might help" / "You could try" / "Something that often works"

### Avoid:
- "deal with", "handle", "manage" (implies control, not care)
- "persona" (clinical, cold)
- "AI thinks you should" (AI is a tool, not an authority)
- Absolute certainty ("This will work", "Always do X")
- Diagnostic language ("Your child has X")

---

## Safety guidelines

1. All AI outputs include a safety disclaimer
2. The `escalation_flag` triggers an escalation banner if risk is detected
3. Crisis resources (988, Crisis Text Line) are included when needed
4. The app never positions itself as a replacement for professional care
5. All user data is owner-scoped and never used for training

---

## Future directions (post-MVP)

- Voice note incident logging
- Routine builder with reminders
- Streak/progress indicators (gentle, not gamified)
- Subscription for premium features (unlimited profiles, advanced insights)
- Integration with calendar for routines
- Professional referral recommendations when escalation triggers
